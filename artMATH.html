About the course

This course is first scheduled to be taught Fall ’15 at SHC. It will be cross listed ART 102 Drawing - MTH 183 Contemporary Math. Students will be able to select which course they get credit for.

This page is actively under development. Please email me if you have any thoughts!

Topics

Main topics: almost in the order they will be taught.

  • (Wanda) - how to draw - measuring
  • technical drawing
    • enforces measuring and detail, drawings without perspective will make them want/appreciate perspective when they get it the next week
    • expanded gear works, screws,
    • characterized by measurements and scale (flat) drawings of the object from different profiles.
      •    
  • geometric object drawings.
    • Here are some from the 16th century: http://bibliodyssey.blogspot.com/2012/03/geometric-perspective.html
  • (Tony) - infinity, loosely leading up to perspective, briefly discuss the connection to religion
  • (Wanda) - perspective - checker patterns on floors
  • (Tony) - Fantastic Example mosaic tile patterns, tilings, symmetry
  • (Wanda) - adv. drawing with perspective
  • (Tony) - Fibonacci
    • Vi Hart - Fibonacci & pinecones https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ahXIMUkSXX0&feature=kp
  • (Wanda) - scientific drawing
      natural high detail representations. Sibley’s birds,  Butterflies, plants
    • follow how-to-draw guides: Flea, Mosquito, Salmon, Angel Fish, beetle, Crab :: http://edtech2.boisestate.edu/bretcrane/502/webquest/intro.html
    • Send out into nature to do find own object.
  • (Tony)
    • hyperbolic space, escher disks, tessellations:
    • http://euler.slu.edu/escher/index.php/Hyperbolic_Geometry
  • (Wanda) drawing tessellations

    • misc other math+art topics: order inconsequental

  • origami
    • http://erikdemaine.org/curved/
    • students produce different origami objects to sketch (or sketch from pictures)
    • watch videos of math in origami:
      • http://courses.csail.mit.edu/6.849/fall10/lectures/L01.html
      • http://vimeo.com/47502276
  • Jordan curves
    • short discussion on proofs and into to the theorem
    • perhaps the best Jordan curve could be done as an installation in salt: http://www.motoi.biz/english/e_top/e_top.html
  • Fractals!
    • They can draw by hand
    • Also write little programs to make animations (see below - hit 'run' to play it)
    • http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Von_Koch_curve
  • students could draw action shots:
    • https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=NoK79YmbGao
    • Perhaps even do an installation.
  • show and tell:
    • research artist or mathematician, and present to class.
    • some links to get their internet warmed up:
    • http://s-i-m-p-l-i-c-i-t-y.org/index.html
    • http://ceblog.sva.edu/tag/philip-ording/

    • Group Projects:

  • Felice Varini - projective geometry
    • make room with tape on wall as a group.
    • draw results from different vantages in the room.
    • https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=2_P-8rArDnE#t=47
  • shadow box
    • https://anilaagha.see.me/yearinreview2013
    • make one and draw sketches of it
  • string shadows
    • Inspired by TV show ‘Dexter’
    • Instructions: Take two stands. The top of one stand acts as a light source. The second stand holds an object to be projected. Run yarn from the ‘light source’ adjacent to the ‘object’ and tape to the wall.  The outline of the sting displays the boundary of the shadow.
